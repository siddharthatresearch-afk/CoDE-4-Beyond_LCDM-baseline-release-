# Effective Dark Energy Density Reconstruction

## Overview

One of the central phenomenological interpretations of the CoDE-4 framework is the reconstruction of an effective dynamical dark-energy density sector.

Although the framework was not constructed from a fundamental scalar-field theory, the bounded deformation structure naturally permits an effective fluid interpretation through reconstruction of a modified vacuum-energy density profile.

The purpose of this derivation is therefore to:
- reconstruct the effective dark-energy density sector,
- analyze its perturbative structure,
- evaluate asymptotic recovery behavior,
- and connect the deformation profile directly to late-time cosmological expansion evolution.

The framework currently behaves as:
- a weakly dynamical effective vacuum-deformation cosmology framework.

---

# Standard Vacuum Energy Structure

In standard flat $\Lambda$CDM cosmology, the vacuum-energy density is constant:

$$
\rho_\Lambda = \mathrm{constant}
$$

leading to the standard expansion equation:

$$
H^2(z) = H_0^2 \left[ \Omega_m(1+z)^3 + \Omega_r(1+z)^4 + \Omega_\Lambda \right]
$$

This corresponds to:
- a perfectly constant vacuum-energy sector,
- and an equation of state:
$$
w = -1
$$
throughout cosmological evolution.

# CoDE-4 Vacuum Reconstruction

The CoDE-4 framework modifies the effective vacuum sector through the bounded deformation function:

$$
C(z) = 1 + \epsilon \left(\frac{z}{(1+z)^2}\right)\left(1-\frac{0.15}{1+z}\right)
$$

The effective dark-energy density is reconstructed phenomenologically as:

$$
\rho_X(z) = \rho_\Lambda C(z)
$$

where:
- $\rho_\Lambda$ represents the baseline vacuum-energy density,
- and $C(z)$ acts as the bounded perturbative deformation profile.

The framework therefore behaves as:
- a weakly evolving effective vacuum-energy sector.
